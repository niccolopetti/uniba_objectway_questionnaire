Biases

This section contains definitions about the biases present in the questionnaire

Overconfidence

Overconfidence bias occurs when people become too confident about their knowledge and skills when estimating investment risks which can lead to making biased judgments resulting in poor returns. read more

Mental Accounting

Mental accounting refers to the different values a person places on the same amount of money, based on subjective criteria, often with detrimental results. To avoid the mental-accounting bias, individuals should treat money as completely interchangeable no matter where they allocate it, whether to a budget account (everyday living expenses), a discretionary spending account, or a wealth account (savings and investments). read more

Herding

Herding occurs when people tend to imitate the judgments of others, which can lead to market bubbles. An investor who exhibits herding tendencies is likely to follow the crowd instead of his own analysis.read more

Representativeness

Representativeness occurs when individuals use stereotypes or draw conclusions from small samples, leading them to misclassify investments as good investments. read more

Anchoring

Anchoring is a heuristic in behavioral finance that describes the subconscious use of irrelevant information, such as the purchase price of a security, as a fixed reference point (or anchor) for making subsequent decisions about that security. Thus, people are more likely to estimate the value of the same item higher if the suggested sticker price is $100 than if it is $50. read more

Disposition

The disposition effect is the tendency of investors to hold losers too long and sell winners too soon. Investors are willing to realize gains but are reluctant to realize losses read more

Financial Literacy

This section contains definitions about the financial literacy heuristics present in the questionnaire

Financial Behavior

Financial Behavior measures the quality of the actions carried out by a subject in the financial field. 

Financial Attitude

Financial Attitude meaures the quality of the mental view the subject has regarding the financial field. 

Investment Decisions

This section contains definitions about the root causes of investment decisions present in the questionnaire

Self-image/firm image coincidence

This heuristic meaures how much you take into consideration the coincidence of yourself and the firm you are investing into, i.e how important it is that it shares your vision and ideals to motivate an investment decision

Neutral information

This heuristic measures how much you take into consideration neutral information (i.e. 
coverage on the press/ government holdings/ economic crysis/etc.)  when you take an investment decision.

Accounting information

This heuristic measures how much you take into consideration technical indicators (performance of a stock/ expected earnings/ etc.) when you take an investment decision

Advocate information


This heuristic measures how much you take into consideration the advices given to you by other people when you take an investment decision 

Personal financial needs

This heuristic measures how much you take into consideration your personal financial needs when you take an investment decision, (the need for example of having liquid money available for a purchase or a need of having a diversificate portfolio)

Personality Traits

This section contains definitions about the Personality Traits present in the questionnaire

Openness

Openness to experience distinguishes people who prefer variety from those who require closure and who gain comfort in their association with familiar people and things. People who consistently seek out different and varied experiences would score high on openness to experience.

Conscientiousness

Conscientiousness describes people who are ordered, controlled, organized, ambitious, achievement-focused, and self-disciplined.

Extraversion

People with high extraversion tend to be affectionate, jovial, talkative, joiners, and fun-loving.

Agreeableness

People who score in the direction of agreeableness tend to be trusting, generous, yielding, acceptant, and good-natured.

Neuroticism

People who score high on Neuroticism are emotionally
sensitive; they become upset easily and frequently
experience negative emotions.
